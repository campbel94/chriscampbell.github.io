Background

My first few years out of college were largely spent working in digital programmatic advertising, on both the buying and the selling side. During that time I was hands-on in a variety of different DSPs and DMPs as well as doing some web debugging. At Adobe my interests and job function became more Analytics focused. In addition to Ad Cloud, I supported Adobe Analytics and the integration between both products. Eventually my scope and title grew to include Adobe Experience Manager (Adobe’s CMS) as well.

My experience with these products gave context to, and propelled my interest in data science. Some examples (which barely scratch the surface) include Ad Cloud’s ML powered bidding algorithm to strategically serve ads to high value audiences and conversion attribution analyses. Adobe Experience Platform’s real-time customer profiling and ‘customer journey orchestration’ tools utilize artificial intelligence. Adobe Analytics alone collects and warehouses on-site user activity data which can then be visualized, placed in a dashboard and analyzed with anomaly detection overlayed. Those descriptions obviously don’t do the technology justice but are exhilarating to wrap your head around.

The majority of my raw data engineering, visualization and Machine Learning skills come from about a year's worth of independent courses and project work. I like to investigate topics that interest me while implementing data cleaning and EDA best-practices as well as ETL workflows where applicable. Admittedly, I love the process of collecting and preprocessing data. I continue to focus on improving my working knowledge of supervised and unsupervised models, and the use cases for each.
